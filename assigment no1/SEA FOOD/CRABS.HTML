DESCRIPTION

Crabs are crustaceans found in oceans, seas, and freshwater habitats worldwide. They are known for their hard exoskeletons, which they periodically shed as they grow. Crabs have ten legs, with the front pair often modified into claws, or "chelae," which they use for defense, feeding, and communication. These decapod crustaceans are omnivorous, feeding on a variety of foods such as algae, mollusks, small fish, and even detritus. They are prized in many cuisines for their sweet and succulent meat, enjoyed in dishes like crab cakes, crab bisque, and crab legs with butter. Crabs play a crucial role in marine ecosystems as both predators and prey, contributing to the balance of coastal and aquatic food webs.
